# Emergency Guidance

> ⚠️ DEMO DATA — have a dentist review this guidance before going live.

## Important Notice

This guidance is general first-aid information only. The AI assistant cannot diagnose the
cause of an emergency. When in doubt, seek professional help immediately.

## When to Seek Immediate Emergency Care

Go to the nearest hospital emergency department or call local emergency services right away
if you have:

- Bleeding that does not stop after 15–20 minutes of firm pressure
- Swelling of the face, jaw, or neck that affects breathing or swallowing
- A severe facial or jaw injury
- High fever together with facial swelling
- Unconsciousness or serious trauma

## Knocked-Out Tooth (First Aid)

- Hold the tooth by the crown (the white part), not the root
- If dirty, rinse briefly with milk or saline — do not scrub
- Try to place it back in the socket, or keep it in milk
- Reach a dentist within 30–60 minutes if possible

## Severe Toothache (Before You Reach a Dentist)

- Rinse with warm salt water
- Use a cold compress on the cheek for swelling
- Do not place aspirin directly on the gum
- Book an urgent appointment; do not self-prescribe antibiotics or strong painkillers

## Broken Tooth or Lost Filling

- Save any broken pieces and bring them with you
- Cover sharp edges with dental wax or sugar-free gum as a temporary measure
- Book an appointment soon to prevent further damage

## Bleeding After an Extraction

- Bite firmly on clean gauze for 20–30 minutes
- Do not rinse forcefully or spit for the first 24 hours
- If bleeding continues heavily, contact the clinic or visit an emergency department
